Slick Speed: Post Mortem

March 1, 2026

I had 10 days to make a game for the Bigmode Game Jam 2026 with the theme "Slick", a word used by Videogamedunkey while giving praise to great mechanics or moments in video games. The result is now Slick Speed, a retro styled arcade game with mechanics that reward you for having quick and slick last-second reflexes.

This post was made to highlight important moments during the development process with a post-jam point of view. Enjoy!

Making a 2D game

For this game jam, I wanted to try something different than my other projects. I've always created games in Unity 3D and although I love creating mechanics in 3D space, I wanted to experiment making pixel art and developing gameplay in a 2D space with a fixed camera. Textures definitely do not need to have a pixelated look for 2D games but after playing Animal Well last summer I was inspired to make a game using pixel art.

Game jams are always a great place to learn new things, and thankfully using Unity's 2D engine went pretty smoothly. I definitely had some trouble at first to make sure the rendering settings were set properly for the pixel art that I made, but once I figured that out, it was easy to know the scale of my game objects. I picked 64 as the pixel per unit base, so a height of 64 pixels for a given asset meant a height of 1 unit in the engine and same for the width.

I made the pixel art and animations for Slick Speed with Asesprite. This was the first time I used this tool and learning the shortcuts was really fun as that sped up the iterating process for the assets I wanted to include in the game. Drawing concept art on a piece of paper and bringing them to life in a game is one of the most satisfying things for me in game development.

Planning

The planning that went into Slick Speed was much better for this game jam compared to my previous game jam game: Graffiti Trials. The biggest mistake with Graffiti Trials during the jam was that I did not put sound or much UI in the game itself on release because my scope was too big for a 7 day jam where I worked my day job during the week. With the Bigmode Game Jam starting on a Thursday and lasting 10 days, I took Thursday and Friday to brainstorm, design, and plan what would eventually become Slick Speed on Saturday.

My biggest goal for this jam was to make a game, even if very simple, that looked, felt, and sounded good. Here was my plan to achieve that:

  • Thursday/Friday: Brainstorm, design, plan
  • Saturday: Prototype mechanics
  • Sunday: Finalize mechanics
  • Monday-Wednesday: Polish and balance gameplay loop, bug fixes, playtests
  • Thursday-Friday: Sound/UI, polish
  • Saturday: Final polish and submit game

The plan from Thursday to Wednesday went exactly as I had planned. However, some of the bug fixes and playtesting did also move from Wednesday to Thursday, delaying the sound and user-interface elements to be done mostly Friday and Saturday. This time around, I made sure to include days just for sound, user-interface, and extra polishing as lessons learned from Graffiti Trials.

Feedback

The feedback for the game once the voting period started blew my expectations. Slick Speed had a very simple way of interacting with the player character. To move, you just press the arrow keys or the traditional W, A, S, or D keys. Players have commented that they were surprised at the overall gameplay with minimal controls and the game's final concept. You can find the rating and feedback for Slick Speed here.

My goal for Slick Speed, especially because of the 10 day constraint, was to create a game that required just one hand to play and only the movement keys I talked about earlier. This created for other gameplay interactions within the game such as the obstacles hitting each other and deviating from their original paths. This interaction between obstacles was interesting to make, but I did not expect it to be one of everyone's favorite moments and, honestly, that was just a product of me messing around with velocity in the collision code. I'm truly grateful to receive so many positive comments on Slick Speed.

Cut Content

Due to the nature of game jams, a lot of content that was originally planned did not make it into the game. For Slick Speed, there were many planned features that I really wanted to include.

One of the planned features was a power-up that appeared on the screen at some point which players could use to slow down time, trace a path within the allowed movement locations and, once the path was confirmed, took the player from the original point of the power up to the path's end point, splitting any obstacles along the path. This was actually the first mechanic that inspired the game and the grid-like movement.

Another feature was using the slick points gained to shop for different skins in a store. I wanted to design different assets for the player character that included different colors and accessories. The shop would have served to reward the player for being slick while playing and to add more variety in the game.

There were also different obstacles which I designed for different levels! Sadly, they only made it on the composition book and not the game. What's next?

My goal for Slick Speed now is to include the mechanics and content that had to be cut due to the time constraint. As I develop and add this content, I will think of a short story, boss fights, and fresh new areas for players to explore.

It was an honor to participate in the Bigmode Game Jam and I loved playing some of the games submitted. I'll be posting updates on X as I develop, follow me there to stay tuned! See ya.
